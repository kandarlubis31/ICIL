# 📐 06 — Layout Composition

> 🔴 Advanced | Prereq: 01 | ~3 min

Composition = arranging elements for hierarchy, eye guidance, and visual storytelling. Beyond grids — it's art.

---

## 6.1 Visual Hierarchy Tools

| Tool | Effect | Application |
|------|--------|------------|
| **Size** | Larger = more important | Hero > section > body |
| **Position** | Top-left = seen first | Key info top-left |
| **Contrast** | High contrast = standout | CTA vs background |
| **Whitespace** | More space = perceived importance | Premium feel |

---

## 6.2 Z-Pattern (Low-Density: Landing Pages, Heroes)

```
1 (Logo) ──────────→ 2 (Nav)
                      ↓
3 (Supporting) ←──── 4 (PRIMARY CTA ← most strategic spot!)
```

## 6.3 F-Pattern (High-Density: Articles, Search, Dashboards)

```
1 ────────────────────→ 2
├────→ 3
├────→ 4
↓ (vertical scan continues)
```

> Place important content at TOP and LEFT. Users rarely read bottom-right.

---

## 6.4 Symmetry vs Asymmetry

| Style | Vibe | Best For |
|-------|------|----------|
| **Symmetrical** | Formal, stable, calm | Corporate, luxury |
| **Asymmetrical** | Dynamic, modern, creative | Startups, portfolios |

## 6.5 Rule of Thirds

Divide into 3×3 grid. Place CTAs/headlines at the 4 **power points** (inner corners). Center = weakest position.

---

## 6.6 Swiss Design Principles

| Principle | Apply |
|-----------|-------|
| Strict grid | Mathematical grid base |
| Asymmetric | Controlled imbalance |
| Sans-serif | Helvetica-style |
| Large visuals | Minimal text |
| Less is more | Remove everything unnecessary |
