# ✍️ 01 — UX Writing Foundations

> 🟢 Beginner | Prereq: — | ~3 min

UX Writing = words in interfaces (buttons, labels, errors, notifications) that make products usable, understandable, and human.

---

## 1.1 UX Writing ≠ Copywriting

| | UX Writing | Copywriting |
|---|-----------|------------|
| Goal | Help users complete tasks | Persuade / sell |
| Focus | Usability & clarity | Conversion & desire |
| Emotion | Calm, helpful, reassuring | Exciting, urgent, aspirational |
| Example | "Password must be 8+ characters" | "Sign up now, FREE!" |

---

## 1.2 6 Core Principles

| Principle | Rule | ❌ Bad | ✅ Good |
|-----------|------|--------|---------|
| **Clear** | No jargon, acronyms | "Authentication failed. Error 403." | "Wrong password. Try again." |
| **Concise** | Cut unnecessary words | "Click the button below to proceed." | "Continue" |
| **Useful** | Every word helps decide/understand | "An error occurred." | "Connection lost. Check internet." |
| **Consistent** | One term across product | "Sign Up" / "Register" / "Daftar" | "Sign Up" everywhere |
| **Conversational** | Write like a human | "Input data into available fields." | "Fill in your name here." |
| **Action-Oriented** | Start with verb | "Submit" | "Send Message", "Create Account" |

---

## 1.3 BLUF Structure (Bottom Line Up Front)

```
1. WHAT happened → "Your card was declined."
2. WHY (if helpful) → "Insufficient funds."
3. SOLUTION → "Try another card or contact your bank."
```

## 1.4 Reading Patterns

Users scan, don't read. F-pattern (text-heavy) or Z-pattern (visual pages).
> Microcopy must be understandable when SCANNED.

| Bad Copy | Burden | Good Copy |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Technical jargon | Extra processing | Everyday language |
| Long sentences | Reading time ↑ | Short & direct |
| Ambiguous | User guesses | Specific & clear |
| Inconsistent | User re-learns | Predictable |

---

## ⚡ Action Checklist
- [ ] Audit: every button starts with a verb (not "Submit")
- [ ] Scan all copy at 2× speed — can you understand it?
- [ ] One glossary term per concept — no synonyms across the product
- [ ] Apply BLUF to all error messages: WHAT + WHY + SOLUTION
